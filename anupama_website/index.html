Anupama Sharma

I am a computational scientist who recently completed my Ph.D. in Computational and Theoretical Sciences from the Central University of Punjab. My thesis, titled "Structure and Dynamics of Complex Liquids Inside Confined Environment", explores the unique behavior of liquids at the molecular level when confined, where structural ordering can significantly affect their dynamics.

I have worked extensively on hydrogen-bonded molecules under confinement and analyzed their effect on low-frequency spectra and hydrogen bond dynamics. I also studied the dispersion mechanisms of carbon nanotubes in different ionic liquids. More recently, I evaluated 13 force field–water model combinations for intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs), identifying the best-performing pair based on NMR data. Currently, I am exploring IDP–small molecule interactions using diffusion models.

Research Interests

  • Molecular Recognition and Spectroscopy
  • Force Field Parameterization and Development in MD
  • Machine Learning in Biomolecular Simulation
  • IDPs and Drug Interactions
